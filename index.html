How Orc names work

Orc names in Dungeons & Dragons and fantasy generally are short, hard, and guttural — built for shouting across a battlefield: Grishnak, Thok, Karg, Ugluk, Shautha, Neega. Many orcs also carry an epithet, a title earned through a deed, a scar, or a reputation: Karg the Skullcrusher, Thok Bloodtusk, Ovak the Unbroken. Half-orcs raised among humans often keep an orc first name as a badge of heritage, sometimes paired with a human surname instead of an epithet.

Using these names at your table

  • Players: generate until one lands, then say it out loud — an orc name should feel good to growl. Keep the epithet or drop it depending on how much backstory you want.
  • Dungeon Masters: the 20-name batch instantly fills a war-band, a raiding party, or a list of clan chiefs. The epithets do half your NPC design for you.
  • Writers: an epithet is a story seed — how did she become "the Merciless"? What did he do to earn "Doomhowl"?

How this generator builds names

First names are assembled from orc-styled syllables — prefix, core, and ending for male and female patterns — then filtered to remove awkward letter runs, giving thousands of short, snarling names instead of the same handful you've seen before. Epithets are drawn from a curated pool of earned titles. Everything is generated in your browser; nothing is stored.
